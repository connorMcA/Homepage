HISTORY

Dachshund racing originally started in Austrailia over 40 years ago and soon spread to the United States. The spark that started all the excitement for the races was a 1993 Budweiser commercial that depicted a dachshund race.


Dachshund races have also been used as a fun way to raise money for different charities. One of the first races to be held in the United States was at the UC Davis picnic day. Veterinary students at this school in California put on the derby in order to raise money to raise money so they can pay for exams to get their liscenses. The "Doxie Derby" at the picnic attracts over 50,000 people each year and 96 contestants.

For a more indepth view of the world of Wiener Dog Racing and the competitors within it, check out the documentary Wiener Takes All.
